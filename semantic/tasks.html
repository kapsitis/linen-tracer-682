Continuous integration of semantic data?
Open data (in particular, created by government and municipalities) are often not published on regular basis; their formats may change to reflect current priorities. In IT industry there is is an orderly mechanism to achieve consistent state in the data applications - it is continuous integration. It uses Maven, Jenkins and similar tools to ensure that the data and software match each other without extra cleanup. Semantic Web and RDF (in addition to the regular mechanisms of continuous integration and package managment) can also incorporate meta-level data regarding the data publishing schedules, format requirements, legal limitations.
"Votes and Quotes" - ability to navigate data related to political decision-making.
Parliament in every country produces lots of information that needs additional digesting and added context in order to be easy to consume. One part of the context could be described by ``event and behavior analitics'' - a study of behavioral patterns and outliers regarding the expected trends (for example, people voting against their expected party ideology). Some of these behaviors could be captured by text categorization and labelling; to allow the policy experts write domain-specific queries to filter out interesting events - to display the most relevant data. (We do not expect people who are not experts in the IT to write the general queries as SQL, SPARQL, Elastic).
