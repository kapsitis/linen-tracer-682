Comprehensive Exam (Bonus Material)

This is NOT the part of Discrete Spring2022 course. It outlines the place of this course in a larger picture, study quality and the retention of knowledge later in life.

"Discrete Structures" (in addition to being a study course) serves as a prerequisite to many subsequent courses. If there are any IT students who need to refresh this material, look at the "Review Topics" section above. Prolonged interruptions in someone's study process, being uncertain about the Persistence of Memory (La persistencia de la memoria), changing the study major from some non-IT subject or participating in exchange programs such as ERASMUS - all these things may cause gaps in your knowledge and you may benefit if you address them. Ideally we would offer the IT people a "Comprehensive Exam" to allow some self-assessment and to find out all major gaps. Such exam has not been implemented yet. Similar goals can be achieved by the Computer Science part of GRE (Graduate Record Examination) offered to the students who do some post-graduate studies after earning their Bachelor's degree.

Keep in mind that "Discrete Structures" has the theory topics overlapping with other branches of mathematics and other study topics. They are interdisciplinary and may require related knowledge from other courses. Below are some examples in no particular order:

Topic in Discrete StructuresSimilar topic in some other subject
Asymptotic Bounds, Big-O notation Limits and comparisons among infinite large functions in Real Analysis or Calculus
Algorithmic procedures in number theory or graphs. Algorithm efficiency in Data Structures and Algorithms.
Binary relations Overriding comparisons, equality and other operators when using data structure libraries for storage or sorting the data.
Binary trees, Full n-ary trees Binary search trees, heaps etc. in Data Structures and Algorithms
Modular Arithmetic, congruences Hashing functions in Data Structures and Algorithms; checksums and error protection in Computer Networks
Euler theorem, discrete logarithms RSA and Diffie Hellman public key methods in Cryptography
Graphs and trees Document object models and layouts in Visualization classes
Random variables, expected value, variance Summarizing data in Descriptive Statistics
Event probabilities, conditional probabilities Risk management topics in Economics and Quantitative Decision Making
Deduction in Boolean and Predicate Logic Functional and Type-oriented Programming

A good comprehensive exam would take into account such cases when there is an interplay between multiple subjects, and it is possible to ask useful (and not too technically difficult) questions involving different knowledge realms.
