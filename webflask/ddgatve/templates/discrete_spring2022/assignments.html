Comprehensive Exam

To assess the knowledge of somebody in "Discrete Mathematics" we can provide a list of topics for self-learning. Eventually this could serve as the curriculum for a comprehensive written exam covering all major topics, but such exams have not been implemented yet. "Discrete Structures" is the prerequisite to many subsequent classes. The target audience are students who needs to refresh this material, do some remedial work on it, has changed his/her major (previously studied non-IT topics), has changed universities (due to an exchange program such as ERASMUS). Ideally we would offer such people a "Comprehensive Exam" to check their knowledge and to find out all major gaps.

Comprehensive exam offers a compact list of topics (See "Shortlist of Final Training Questions" above) that cover the most important things only. As we develop this "Shortlist", it may become more interdisciplinary to include related IT topics. Such as Calculus (for Big-O calculations), Visualizations (graphs and trees, but also other discrete math topics), Algorithms (in Number Theory), Introductory Cryptography (in Number Theory), Statistics (as it is related to the Probabilities covered in this class), Functional languages and automating proofs (in Propositional and Predicate Logic), Data Structures (in sets and relations subtopic).
