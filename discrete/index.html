Readings and Presentations

Stories about Algorithms

  • Large Primes and Probabilistic Algorithms
    Can include Miller-Rabin primality test, Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search (GIMPS) project, Agrawal–Kayal–Saxena primality test.
  • Secure Hash Functions
    Can include MD5 hash collisions, SHA1, SHA2, SHA3. Password hashing in Web applications and in data storage.
  • Arithmetic Coding
    Can discuss Huffman coding and other entropy-based compressions for message sequences.
  • String Searching
    Naive, Knuth-Moris-Pratt and Boyer-Moore algorithms. Their comparison.
  • Karatsuba or Toom-Cook Multiplication
    Explain one of the fast algorithms to multiply two numbers (can pick something else, but it has to be faster than quadratic.) Apply Master's theorem to solve the recursion and estimate the speed of the algorithm.
  • Lindenmayer systems
    Use recursive rules to generate 2D patterns and also recursive plants.

Data Protection and Privacy

  • Matching Regular Expressions.
    Regex pattern uses, their matching with finite state automata and performance of these matchers, including catastrophic backtracking.
  • The Anatomy of a Credit Card Number.
    How to prevent stealing large numbers of credit card numbers. Luhn check, IINs/BINs - Predicting issuer bank, first digit. Support terms to avoid false positives.
  • File Checksums
    CRC and other "binary fingerprints" of files to avoid file tampering or unintended leaks.
  • Bloom Filters
    Storing large hashtables very efficiently in a probabilistic data structure.
  • Detecting Plagiarism
    Rabin-Carp algorithms of substring matching, N-gram algorithms, Upload filters in EU Copyright Directive and their false positives.

Proofs, Sets and Relations

  • Paradoxes of Self-Reference
    Russel's paradox, diagonalization results, Barber's paradox, Halting problem.
  • Gödel's Incompleteness Theorem
    Results about non-provable, but true statements that exist for each formal system.
  • Arrow's Impossibility Theorem
    Results how voting among shareholders can lead either to a "dictator" deciding everything or to the voting process being easy to manipulate.
  • Creative Strategies in Proofs
    George Polya ("Mathematical Discovery: On Understanding, Learning and Teaching Problem Solving", "Mathematical Discovery", "How to Solve It") and Paul Zeitz ("The Art and Craft of Problem Solving") on developing mathematical creativity.
  • Approximating irrational numbers (roots etc.) with rational ones.
    Examples of well-tempered harmonies; Farey fraction sequences and Stern–Brocot tree, mediant of two rational numbers, continuous fractions and when they are periodic.
  • Cellular Automata

Discrete Probabilities

  • Matrix Games
    Distinguish zero-sum and non-zero-sum games. Show how to find Nash equilibrium; apply it to some simple number guessing games (Rock paper scissors; some variant of a Prisoner's dilemma; simultaneous guessing of positive integers; and High-Low guessing game).
  • Counter-examples in Probabilities
    Various surprises in probability theory: Boy or Girl paradox. Monty Hall problem. Four 6-sided dices that have non-transitive order relationship. Three pairwise independent random events are not necessarily mutually independent.
  • Coin Tossing and Random Walks
    Explain coin-tossing experiment series; how frequently the change of sign happens, how often one player is ahead of another; how far the random walk is expected to get away from the equilibrium.
  • Naive Bayes Classifiers
    Classification of texts based on models showing conditional probabilities of certain words. Show how spam filters can be implemented based on Naive Bayes.
  • Proportional Voting Systems
    D'Hondt or Sainte-Laguë proportional voting systems, their practical consequences in Latvia, Israel, Hungary and elsewhere.
  • Discrete random distributions and their properties
    Mention "canonical" examples and practical applications to Bernoulli distribution, Geometric distribution, Poisson distribution etc.

Stories about Graph Theory

  • Alpha-Beta Pruning in building game playing algorithms.
    Build and analyze a game-tree for a pen-and-paper that is feasible to analyze on a PC computer. Apply alpha-beta pruning algorithm to have a computer that wins human players.
  • Hamiltonian cycles and NP-complete problems.
    Define Hamiltonian cycles. Formulate theorem by Emanuels Grinbergs for Hamiltonian cycles in planar graphs. Define how the NP-complete problems can be reduced to/from Hamiltonian cycles.
  • Computer-based proof of a four-color theorem.
    Formulate four-color theorem (and similar theorems in Torus topologies, etc.). Show how it can break down to analysis of a large number of cases.
  • Google Page Rank Algorithm
    Can include other computable rankings of search results.
  • Maximum Flow in a Directed Graph
    Ford-Fulkerson algorithm or Edmonds-Karp algorithm; Braes's paradox.
